Lead vs. Deal

A critical distinction in the sales process.

L

Lead (Prospect)

A person/company that might buy. Often lives in the "Marketing" stage.

D

Deal (Opportunity)

A qualified potential transaction. Has a dollar amount ($) and a close date.

Custom Objects & Properties

  • Custom Objects: Data unique to your business (e.g., "Shipments", "Subscriptions").
  • Custom Properties: Fields tailored to data needs (e.g., "Preferred Delivery Date").

02. The Sales Pipeline

Visualizing the journey of a Deal. The standard pipeline moves from Qualification to Closed Won, with probability increasing at each step.

Deal Volume by Stage (Funnel)

Simulated data showing the typical attrition rate in a sales funnel.

Insight: The funnel shape visually represents conversion health. A wide top indicates strong lead generation, while a narrow bottom highlights where deals drop off (e.g., between Evaluation and Review).

1. Qualification

Initial vetting. Does the prospect have budget and authority?

2. Initial Evaluation

Discovery calls and demos. The prospect is actively assessing the solution.

3. Quote Issued / Design Reg.

Formal pricing is presented. In complex sales, "Design Registration" locks in specs.

4. Closed Won / Lost

The terminal stages. Won = Revenue booked. Lost = Reason required (e.g., Price, Competitor).
